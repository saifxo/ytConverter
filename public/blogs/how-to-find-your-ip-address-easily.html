Cracking the Code: How to Find Your IP Address Easily

In today's digital world, every device is connected to the internet. These devices are assigned a unique identifier known as an IP address. This IP address of yours plays a crucial role in everything you do online, from browsing websites to streaming videos. An IP address is a set of unique numerical label that helps to route information to and from your device, enabling communication with other devices and access to online resources. It acts as a unique identifier for your device on the internet. However, unlike your home address, your IP address can sometimes feel like a hidden code. Finding your IP address is essential for network troubleshooting, device setup, and understanding your online presence.

Fortunately, finding your IP address is not as difficult as it may seem to you. In this blog, we will explain how to easily find your IP address on different devices. By following the instructions provided, you can quickly locate your IP address, making your online experience smoother.

Understanding IP addresses

Before we get into the details of how to find your IP address, let’s go over some key terms to have a deeper understanding.

  • What is an IP address? An IP address is a unique identifier assigned to devices on a network, including the Internet. It plays a vital role in data transmission, just like a street address helps mail reach your doorstep. There are two main types of IP addresses. IPv4 and IPv6.

    IPv4 is the most commonly used type, consisting of four numbers separated by periods (e.g., 192.168.1.1). On the other hand, IPv6 is a newer version that uses eight groups of four hexadecimal digits (e.g., 2001:0db8:85a3:0000:0000:8a2e:0370:7334).

  • Public IP address. A public IP address is a unique identifier for a network on the internet, assigned by the internet service provider (ISP). It's like your street address in the physical world that allows communication between your network and the wider internet. Each network is allocated a single, distinct public IP address, ensuring no two networks share the same identity on the internet. The ISP assigns public IP addresses and maintains a pool of addresses. Unlike private IP addresses, your public IP addresses are visible and accessible to other devices on the internet, allowing websites and servers to identify your network and send information back to you.
  • Private IP address. Private IP addresses are unique identifiers assigned to each device within a local network by the router, creating a secure internal network. These addresses are not directly accessible from the internet, enhancing security. Unlike your public IPs, private IPs are non-unique on the global stage and are allocated in specific, predefined ranges reserved for internal network use. Multiple networks can utilize the same range of private IP addresses without causing conflicts. Private IP addresses are invisible to the outside world and exist solely within the network. The router automatically assigns private IP addresses to all connected devices using DHCP, ensuring each device has a unique identifier for communication within the network.

How to find your IP address on different devices

  • For Windows

    1. Open the Start menu and look for "Settings."
    2. Click on "Network & Internet" and then select "Wi-Fi."
    3. Choose the network you're connected to and click on "Properties."
    4. Scroll down to find the "IPv4 address" listed next to it.

  • For Mac

    1. Click on the Apple icon in the top-left corner and select "System Preferences."
    2. Go to "Network" and choose the network you're connected to.
    3. Click on the "Advanced" button and then the "TCP/IP" tab.
    4. Your IP address will be displayed next to "IPv4 Address."

  • For Android

    1. Open the "Settings" app and tap on "Wi-Fi."
    2. Long-press on the network you're connected to and select "Network details."
    3. Your IP address will be displayed under "IP address."
